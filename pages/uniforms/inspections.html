Inspections

Civil Air Patrol conducts uniform inspections to ensure cadets maintain proper appearance and professionalism. Inspections are based on the Cadet Uniform Inspection Scorecard (CAPF 60-98), which evaluates several key areas.

Scorecard Categories

  • Personal Appearance: Haircut, grooming, cosmetics, or shave. Clean and professional overall look.
  • Garments: Cleanliness, fit, press, and absence of lint or loose threads. Proper shirt tuck and general uniform presentation.
  • Accoutrements: Correct placement of patches, insignia, and ribbons. Proper alignment of the gig line.
  • Footwear: Clean, polished shoes or boots. Correct boot blousing (if applicable).
  • Military Bearing: Posture, military courtesy, attention, and focus during inspection.

Overall Rating

Cadets are evaluated according to their phase level:

Phase Level Needs Improvement Satisfactory Excellent
Phase I 0-3 pts. 4-5 pts. 6-10 pts.
Phase II-IV 0-4 pts. 5-7 pts. 8-10 pts.

Based on CAPF 60-98, Cadet Uniform Inspection Scorecard (October 2021).
