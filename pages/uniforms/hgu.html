Color Guard (CAPP 60-33)

Primary Role: Presenting and posting the colors (flags) at ceremonies, parades, and competitions.

  • Uniform: Standard CAP blues (Class B or Class A) following grade-specific wear.
  • Drill Source: CAPP 60-33 (Color Guard Drill).
  • Focus areas: Competitions (wing/region/national), community posting/retrieving flags.

Honor Guard (CAPP 52-8)


Primary Role: Ceremonial representation of CAP — honoring the flag, nation, and those who served.

  • Uniform: Class A or B with distinctive items regardless of grade (see CAPR 39-1, § 9.6).

  • Distinctions: Service cap, white ascot, white belt, white gloves.
  • Drill Source: CAPP 52-8 HGA Handbook and the USAF Base Honor Guard Manual (with NHQ-directed changes).
  • Focus areas: Exhibition & ceremonial drill, funeral elements, six-person flag fold, drama/tribute elements, colors team overlap.

Fun Fact

CAP Honor Guards trace their style back to old Air Force Military Police flag details, not the modern USAF Honor Guard. CAP maintains traditions that the Air Force has since changed — making us a little cooler.
